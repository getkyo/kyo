# Research finding Q-007

Question: Which of the two Classpath.open overloads is the canonical implementation that the other delegates to?
Type: research-knowable
Budget: 10 minutes (used: 3)

## Answer

The `(roots, strict)` two-parameter overload is canonical. Both overloads already delegate to the private `openImpl`: the no-strict overload calls `openImpl(roots, strict = false)` and the two-parameter overload calls `openImpl(roots, strict)`. The audit finding A2 (audit-findings.md:80) flags that the delegation relationship is not made explicit in the public surface -- concretely, the no-strict overload should call the canonical public overload directly (`open(roots, strict = false)`) instead of calling `openImpl` itself. CONTRIBUTING.md §358-§382 states "simple variants delegate to the canonical implementation, never duplicate logic" and shows the fuller-arity form as canonical with shorter-arity variants delegating into it. The `(roots, strict)` overload is fuller-arity and therefore canonical; the `(roots)` overload becomes its delegate.

All three external callsites in `kyo-tasty-bench` (`TastyQueryCompareBench.scala:141`, `ColdLoadBench.scala:91`, `ColdLoadFullBench.scala:150`) use the no-strict one-argument overload. No external callsite passes an explicit `strict` value. `openCached` follows the same single-body pattern: one public overload delegates to a private `openCachedImpl`; it has no strict parameter and no overload pair, so no canonical-delegation question arises there.
